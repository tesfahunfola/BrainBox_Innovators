ABOUT OUR THINK TANK

BrainBox Innovators is a student project that aims to use technology and entrepreneurship to impact on the African education.

Key features of our Think Tank

MISSION

To reduce the illiteracy rate by increasing the provision of qualitative and quantitative education in Africa through Technology and entrepreneurship.

Problem Solving

Over 2.2 million children are out of school in South Sudan, and as COVID-19 hit the country, another 2 million children were out of school too according to Unicef and UNESCO reports of 2020 and 2022.

OUR APPROACH TO SOLVE THIS ISSUE

BUILD RELATIONSHIPS

We will build partnerships with education concerned organizations: Partner with AL group to help us in the execution process Partnership with the South Sudan ministry of education Partner with UNICEF and UNESCO

AWARENESS

Most parents especially pastoralists don't know the importance of education, of which the keep their kids in cattle camps. Therefore, we need to create awareness projects to educate parents with their children on the importance of education. Implement 150 awareness projects in South Sudan Ensure 1.5 million children have clue about the importance of education.

BUILDING AND PROVISION OF FACILITIES

Building 5 big high schools Building 10 primary schools in rural areas that don't have access to education Providing 10 public libraries to 10 states of South Sudan.

VISION

OUR THINK TANK VISION IN 10 YEARS

To ensure 2.5 million children in South Sudan have access to quality education by 2032 We need to have a community that have every child have right to education Where every youth thinks of what positive value they can bring to their communities. Where entrepreneurship is the way forward Where every child have access to technological facilities.

OUR WORK

ELAB CHALLENGES

The aim of this Elab challenge is to help young leaders to work collaboratively to innovate, create bonds among themselves, most importantly work together for the common good in solving their communal matters through innovation.

ELAB CHALLENGE ONE

In challenge One there was a series of riddles we had to solve with our think tank and then we had to pinpoint the exact place on the ALU campus. Finally, we took pictures and videos that show our journey. It was an amazing challenge to explore the campus.

ELAB CHALLENGE TWO

Every young person who is impact-driven on the African continent has a big challenge to solve. They must be willing to use their skills and passions to solve African greatest problems. Accordingly, in this challenge, we explored individualistic passions, why they are passionate about them, how they can solve or achieve their passions and what they can bring to their team.

ELAB CHALLENGE THREE

The dramatic climatic changes around the world have put some the humanitarian red flags in the lives of innocent people worldwide. Due to prolonged drought and recent flooding in the country, the natural disasters left millions of people displaced and put children in danger due to a shortage of food items. Many international agencies are trying their best to curb the nutrition crisis. Still, the dramatic climate change is increasing dramatically, and there's a need to take drastic actions in supporting organizations and the government of Madagascar to rescue the innocents from suffering.

In the video below, we have highlighted the number of people affected, reports from several NGOs, the present, and the future situations, and the need for action.

ELAB CHALLENGE FOUR

BrainBox Innovators took challenge to the community. Our campaign was on community awareness, especially for parents to ensure they are a concrete support system for their children in pursuit of their future. More to come on your way in few weeks to come.
